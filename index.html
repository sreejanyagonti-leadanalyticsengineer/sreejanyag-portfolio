How I work

My professional architecture

The mental models that show up in how I actually approach problems, lead teams, and ship things.

Before writing any code, I ask:
What decision does this enable? If I can't name the business decision this supports, I go back and ask. Code without a decision context is often wasted effort.
On data quality:
Data quality is not a data engineering problem. It's a trust problem. If stakeholders don't trust the data, they build shadow spreadsheets — and the pipeline becomes irrelevant.
On communicating with stakeholders:
30-second version first, details on request. They want to know: is the data right? When is it ready? What do they need to do? I answer those three, then stop.
On leading a team:
I optimize for clarity over activity. A team that knows exactly what they're building and why ships faster than a busy team that's unclear. My job is to remove ambiguity.
On architecture decisions:
"What's the simplest thing that works at the scale we actually have, and what's the first sign we'd need to revisit it?" I put a monitoring tripwire there.
From every production incident:
Assumptions that went untested are where production breaks. My rule: if I catch myself saying "this will definitely work," I write a test for it.
